Layers of the Old Dock

Layers of the Old Dock is a digital artwork created by Simon Bowen and Tim Shaw as part of a collaboration between Newcastle University and the Foundation for Art and Creative Technology (FACT). This artwork was sponsored by Liverpool ONE and is inspired by the collections of National Museums Liverpool.

Media Credits

This artwork uses material from the International Slavery Museum, Merseyside Maritime Museum , the exhibition On Their Own - Britain’s Child Migrants (a collaboration between the Australian National Maritime Museum and National Museums Liverpool), and various public art collections.

Text

“I appear before this immense assembly as a thief and a robber. I stole this head, these limbs, this body from my master and ran off with them.” Frederick Douglass, former slave and abolition campaigner, 1853
- Displayed in the International Slavery Museum.

“When we got to the docks my dad said ‘let’s look at the big liners’ and the one he picked on was the Montclare… My dad and mum said ‘let’s go on board and have a look around’… but unknown to me that was the start of our new life; dad said we were going for a ride and after quite a few days we arrived at Pictou Harbour, Nova Scotia.” J Clark
- Displayed in the Merseyside Maritime Museum

"I leaned over the railing and watched the ropes which held us to the dock. When they slipped into the water my heart sank with them." Mavis Appleyard
"I told my mother about a scheme to send children to Australia. She said, ‘Although it will be dangerous we won’t stand in your way. In any case you may have a better future there’." Bill Nicholson
"That first night I was not the only one who cried for the ship, for Mum, for England and everyone back there." Anonymous
- Personal testimonies from: On Their Own - Britain’s Child Migrants
